# Curtain Lining FAQ

**Do curtains come with lining by default?**
No — curtains are unlined by default. Lining is a made-to-order add-on.

**How much shorter is the lining than the curtain?**
Finished lining sits about 1.25" shorter than the finished curtain length — this is the industry standard, meant to keep the lining from peeking below the hem.

**Should I add lining to sheer curtains?**
Not recommended — it adds weight/bulk that works against the sheer's light, airy look and reduces the natural light it lets in. For more light control without full lining, consider semi-sheer or linen curtains instead.

**What's the care routine for lined curtains?**
Dry clean is recommended for all lined curtains. If machine washing, use a gentle cycle and wash one panel at a time.

**Why add lining at all?**
Unlined curtains fade and sun-damage faster and offer only the base fabric's own light-blocking level. Lining adds body/weight, protects against fading, and increases light control/privacy/insulation depending on which lining is chosen.

Full lining specs (weight, color, exact light-blocking ratio per lining) are in [Lining Type](../guides/lining-type.md).
