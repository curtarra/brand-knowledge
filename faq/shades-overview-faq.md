# Shades Overview FAQ

**What types of custom shades does Curtarra offer?**
Roman shades (cordless, motorized, or no-drill), roller shades (cordless or motorized), and woven wood/bamboo shades — all made to the customer's exact window size, in a range of fabrics, colors, and linings.

**What's the difference between Roman shades and roller shades?**
Roman shades fold into soft fabric pleats when raised — a warmer, more elegant look, but a larger stack back (6–18"). Roller shades wrap around a tube — a sleeker, more minimal look with a much smaller stack back (2–4"). Roman for a cozy aesthetic; roller for a space-saving profile.

**Are custom shades worth it over off-the-shelf?**
Cut to the exact window dimensions for a fit, light control, and finish that stock sizes can't match, plus more fabric/lining/operating-system choice.

**Which rooms suit which shade type?**
Roller → bedrooms, offices, bathrooms (sleek, easy-clean). Roman → living/dining rooms (soft, elegant). Cordless blackout → nurseries/kids' rooms. Motorized → tall, hard-to-reach, or large windows.

**Are Curtarra's shades safe for children and pets?**
Curtarra states its shades are cordless and meet the ANSI/WCMA A100.1-2018 safety standard, removing the dangling-cord strangulation/entanglement risk.

**What lining options exist for Roman shades?**
White Lining (140 GSM, ~30% light-blocking), Cotton Lining (137 GSM, ~30%), White Blackout Lining (240 GSM, 100%), or unlined — the same lining products documented in [Lining Type](../guides/lining-type.md).

**What does lining add to a Roman shade?**
Privacy, light control up to full blackout, thermal insulation, UV protection for the face fabric, and a more tailored/weighted drape. Trade-off: a bulkier, larger stack back and slightly reduced natural light even with a light-filtering liner.

**Are blackout shades good for nurseries/bedrooms?**
Yes — they block 99%+ of light, supporting consistent sleep/nap schedules. Pair with an outside mount extending 2–3" beyond the frame for maximum blockage.

**How do I layer a shade with curtains?**
Mount the shade inside the window recess as the primary light-control layer, then hang decorative curtains on a wall-mounted rod outside the recess for texture and a designer layered look.
