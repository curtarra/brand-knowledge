# Shades Installation, Operation & Troubleshooting FAQ

## Installation

**Basic install steps:** mark bracket positions with a pencil and level → drill pilot holes (use wall anchors for drywall) → secure the mounting brackets → snap/click the headrail into the brackets → attach the valance if included → test raise/lower several times. Windows over 48" wide should add a center support bracket.

**No-drill Roman shades:** use a tension system (headrail expands against the frame) or an adhesive system (sticks to the frame top) instead of screws. Designed for inside mount on rigid frames (metal, vinyl, concrete) — not recommended for soft/decorative wood frames, very large/heavy shades, or outside mount.

## Operation

**Cordless mechanism:** spring-loaded roller + friction lock. Push the bottom rail up to raise (stores spring energy); pull down to lower (releases energy gradually). Locks at any height, no cords.

**Motorized mechanism:** quiet built-in motor on a rechargeable lithium battery (lasts ~3–4 months per charge; no hardwiring or electrician needed). Controlled via the included remote or hands-free through Alexa, Google Assistant, or Apple HomePod (Matter-compatible, no extra hub required). One remote can control up to 5 shades on separate channels.

**Motorized shade warranty:** 1 year, no-questions-asked replacement for any non-human-caused issue.

## Troubleshooting

**Shade won't raise or lower after mounting:** usually a bracket sits too close to the internal cord lock/lift mechanism, or the two brackets are at mismatched height/depth, putting the headrail out of level and creating friction. Loosen the affected bracket and shift it slightly. Tip for next time: hold the assembled headrail up to the window frame first to mark true bracket position, rather than measuring and drilling independently.

**Bottom bar won't sit flat / looks uneven:** usually the internal weight bar shifted during shipping. Hang the shade, manually raise it until the bottom 2–3 pleats gather, then pull down with a bit more force than usual to reseat the bottom bar fully. Contact support@curtarra.com for a demo video if it's still not flat.

**Motorized remote not responding:** confirm batteries are seated correctly and the battery pull-tab insulator has been removed (the most commonly missed step). If that's not it, re-pair the remote per its manual. Still stuck — contact support@curtarra.com.

**Installation videos:** available on the How-To-Videos page. Worth watching specifically for ceiling mounts and motorized components — these two categories generate the most confusion from written steps alone.
